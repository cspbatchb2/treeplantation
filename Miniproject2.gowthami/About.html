History Of Tree Plantation

Tree plantation has a long history dating back to ancient times. It has been practiced for various reasons, including environmental, social, and economic benefits. In ancient civilizations, tree planting was significant for providing shade, fruits, and timber. One notable historical event related to tree plantation is the Arbor Day. Arbor Day is a holiday in which individuals and groups are encouraged to plant and care for trees. The first Arbor Day was celebrated in Spain in the late 16th century. It was later popularized in the United States in the 19th century by J. Sterling Morton, who advocated for tree planting to improve the environment.
